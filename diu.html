📍 Location

Diu is a coastal town situated on an island in the Arabian Sea, part of the union territory of Dadra and Nagar Haveli and Daman and Diu. Connected to mainland India by the Fudam Bridge, it is approximately 200 kilometers from Rajkot in Gujarat.

📚 History & Importance

Diu was a Portuguese colony until 1961, and its unique architecture beautifully reflects European influence. The town served as a major trading port and military stronghold during the colonial era. This blend of Indian and Portuguese cultures makes it a fascinating destination for history enthusiasts.

✨ Features

Diu features clean, well-maintained beaches with crystal-clear waters perfect for swimming and water sports.

  • Nagoa Beach - Crescent-shaped beautiful beach with water activities
  • Diu Fort - 16th-century Portuguese fort with impressive architecture
  • St. Paul's Church - Beautiful Indo-Portuguese architectural masterpiece
  • Naida Caves - Ancient rock caves with historical significance

🎯 Why Visit Diu?

Diu is perfect for a peaceful beach vacation with minimal crowds. It offers an ideal blend of relaxation, history, and natural beauty with a laid-back Portuguese-influenced lifestyle.

  • Calm Waters - Safe beaches ideal for swimming and families
  • Historical Sites - Explore colonial architecture and ancient monuments
  • Local Cuisine - Fresh seafood and fusion Portuguese-Indian dishes
  • Peaceful Atmosphere - Away from crowded beach destinations
